Levels of Biological Organization
* Life is organized into several levels, from the smallest to the largest:
   * Molecule: The chemical level, including DNA, proteins, and other molecules essential to life.
   * Cell: The basic unit of life.
   * Tissue: Groups of similar cells that perform a specific function.
   * Organ: Structures made up of tissues working together to perform complex functions.
   * Organ System: Groups of organs that perform related functions.
   * Organism: An individual living entity.
   * Population: Groups of individuals of the same species living in a specific area.
   * Community: All the different populations that live together in an area.
   * Ecosystem: The community plus the physical environment.
   * Biosphere: The sum of all ecosystems, representing the zone of life on Earth.
